# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Daira — 22, architecture student in Mexico, busy and stressed, low sleep. Uses this on her **phone** (and sometimes laptop) to: check what to eat each day (desayuno / comida / cena), see exact quantities and macros, and run her **weekly grocery shopping** with a checklist. Must work **offline** (spotty connection at the market / on the go). Her partner Agustín set it up for her.

## Product Purpose

A personal, single-user diet companion. Turns a Python-verified 7-day Mexican meal plan into a daily, glanceable view plus a check-off-as-you-go weekly shopping list with exact quantities. Success = she opens it daily, knows exactly what/how much to eat and buy, and never needs a connection. It must feel supportive and appetizing, never clinical or punishing — she is at a healthy weight working on recomposition and digestion, not a crash diet.

## Brand Personality

Warm, encouraging, homey-but-clean. Three words: **cálido, claro, confiable**. Mexican home-kitchen warmth (comal, limón, talavera) expressed through color/type, not kitsch. Tone is a caring partner, not a doctor.

## Anti-references

- Clinical macro-tracker dashboards (MyFitnessPal density, gray spreadsheets).
- Crash-diet / "shred" aesthetics (aggressive reds, before/after, shouting numbers).
- The beige/cream "AI warm" body background. Warmth comes from a committed terracotta primary + fresh-green accent on clean white surfaces.
- Cheesy clip-art sombrero/cactus Mexicana.

## Design Principles

- **Glance first, detail on tap.** The day's three meals readable in one screen; macros and tips expand.
- **The shopping list is the hero.** Checkable, persistent, exact quantities — the most-used surface; optimize it.
- **Offline is non-negotiable.** All data local; state survives with no connection.
- **Supportive, not punishing.** Celebrate the plan and the treats (chocolate is in the plan), don't scold.
- **Numbers are trustworthy.** Every quantity/macro is Python-computed and verified, presented plainly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large ≥3:1. Spanish (es-MX) UI. Large tap targets (≥44px) for one-handed phone use. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support. Checkboxes operable by keyboard and screen reader.
